## Key Features to Consider

### IP Ratings (Durability)
Look for at least an **IP65 rating** to ensure your lights can withstand heavy rain, snow, and extreme heat.

### Battery Capacity
Measured in mAh, higher capacity ensures the lights stay bright during the long winter nights.

### Connectivity: Wi-Fi vs. Bluetooth vs. Zigbee
*   **Wi-Fi**: Easiest to set up but can drain battery faster.
*   **Bluetooth Mesh**: Great for large groups of lights in a concentrated area.
*   **Zigbee/Bridge**: The most stable for long distances (requires a hub).

## FAQ

### Do solar smart lights work in the winter?
Yes, but they may have shorter run times due to fewer daylight hours. High-quality models in 2026 use "low-power" modes to extend battery life during winter.

### Can I control these with my voice?
Most top brands like Ring, Philips Hue, and Govee are compatible with Alexa, Google Assistant, and Siri.

### How bright are solar smart lights?
Modern solar pathlights range from 50 to 150 lumens, while floodlights can reach 1,000+ lumens.
