## What to Look for in an RV Solar Generator

1.  **Battery Chemistry:** In 2026, never buy an NMC unit for an RV. Always look for **LiFePO4**. They are safer in the heat of a parked RV and last 10+ years.
2.  **Solar Input (Watts):** Look for a unit that can accept at least 400W of solar. This ensures you can actually refill the battery during the limited daylight hours.
3.  **Port Selection:** Ensure the unit has at least one **100W USB-C PD** port for charging modern MacBooks and high-end phones without an adapter.
4.  **Inverter Quality:** Ensure it uses a **Pure Sine Wave** inverter. Modified sine waves can damage sensitive RV electronics like your TV or starlink system.

## FAQ: Portable Solar Generators for RVing

### Can a solar generator run an RV air conditioner?
The **Lipower 6144Wh** can run a standard RV AC for 3-5 hours. The **Jackery 2000 v2** can run one for about 1-2 hours. For extended AC use, you will need a significant permanent solar array on your RV roof.

### How do I charge my generator while driving?
All the units recommended above include a 12V car charger. Simply plug it into your cigarette lighter port, and the generator will charge from your RV’s alternator while you travel.

### Are these units waterproof?
Most units are water-resistant but **not waterproof**. You should keep the generator inside your RV or under a protective cover, while only the solar panels remain outside in the elements.
