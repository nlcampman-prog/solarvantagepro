## What to Look For When Buying

- **Battery Chemistry:** Always look for **LiFePO4** (Lithium Iron Phosphate). They are safer and last 3-5 times longer than standard Lithium-ion batteries.
- **Inverter Size:** Ensure the AC output (Watts) is higher than the "startup" wattage of your most power-hungry appliance (usually the AC or microwave).
- **Solar Input:** Check the maximum solar wattage the unit can accept. Higher solar input means faster recharging when you're off-grid.

## FAQ: Solar for RVs

**Q: Can a solar generator run my RV air conditioner?**
A: Yes, but you need a unit with at least 3000W output and a large battery (like the EcoFlow Delta Pro 3). Even then, you will likely only get 2-4 hours of AC unless you have significant solar input.

**Q: How many solar panels do I need?**
A: For a 2000Wh generator, we recommend at least 400W of solar panels to ensure you can recharge fully in 5-6 hours of sunlight.
