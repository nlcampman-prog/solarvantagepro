## What to Look for in an RV Solar Generator

1.  **Battery Type:** In 2026, always look for **LiFePO4** (Lithium Iron Phosphate). They last 3-5 times longer than standard Lithium-ion batteries.
2.  **Inverter Size:** Ensure the AC output (Watts) is higher than the "surge" wattage of your most power-hungry appliance.
3.  **Solar Input:** Check the maximum solar wattage the unit can accept. Higher solar input means faster recharging when you're off-grid.
4.  **Weight vs. Portability:** If you plan to move the unit in and out of your RV, look for units with wheels or ergonomic handles.

## FAQ: Portable Solar Generators for RVing

**Q: Can a solar generator run an RV air conditioner?**
A: Yes, but you will need a large unit like the EcoFlow Delta Pro (3600W+) and a significant solar array to keep it running for more than a few hours.

**Q: How many solar panels do I need for my RV?**
A: For a 2kWh generator, we recommend at least 400W of solar panels to ensure a full recharge in 5-7 hours of direct sunlight.

**Q: Can I charge my solar generator while driving?**
A: Absolutely. Most modern units can charge via your RV's 12V cigarette lighter port or a dedicated DC-to-DC charger.
