## Understanding Your CPAP Power Needs

Before choosing a generator, you need to know how much "juice" your machine consumes. Most modern CPAP machines (like the ResMed AirSense 11 or Philips DreamStation 2) consume between **30W and 90W** depending on your settings.

**The "Heated" Factor:**
The biggest power drain is the heated humidifier and heated tubing. Turning these off can reduce your power consumption by up to 75%, allowing a smaller generator to last multiple nights.

| Setting | Estimated Power Draw | Hours per 300Wh Battery |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Standard (No Heat)** | 10W - 15W | 20 - 25 Hours (3+ Nights) |
| **Heated Humidifier** | 40W - 60W | 5 - 7 Hours (1 Night) |
| **Heated Tubing + Humidifier** | 70W - 90W | 3 - 4 Hours (Half Night) |

## The "Pro Tip": Use a DC-to-DC Adapter
Most CPAP machines run on DC power internally. When you plug them into a standard AC outlet on your generator, the generator has to convert DC to AC, and then your machine's brick converts it back to DC. This double conversion wastes about 15-20% of your battery.

**Buy a 12V DC power cord for your specific CPAP model.** This allows you to plug directly into the generator's "cigarette lighter" port, significantly extending your battery life.
