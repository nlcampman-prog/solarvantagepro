## What to Look For in a Home Backup Solar Generator

When investing in a solar generator to back up your home, do not just look at the price. Pay close attention to these three core technical specifications:

1.  **Capacity (Wh/kWh):** This measures how much total electrical energy the battery can store. For essential circuits (refrigerator, lights, router, phones), you need a minimum of **3 kWh**. For whole-home backup (including heavy appliances like water heaters, well pumps, or central AC), you need **10 kWh to 25 kWh**.
2.  **Output (Watts/kW):** This is the maximum amount of power the generator can continuously deliver at any one second. To run heavy-duty 240V appliances (well pumps, dryers, central AC), you need a generator that outputs at least **3,600W to 7,200W**.
3.  **Battery Chemistry (LiFePO4 vs. NMC):** In 2026, never buy an NMC-battery generator. Demand **Lithium Iron Phosphate (LiFePO4)** battery cells. LiFePO4 cells are incredibly safe, highly thermal-resistant, and last for over **3,500 to 4,000 complete charge cycles** (roughly 10 to 15 years of daily use) before losing any noticeable capacity.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can a solar generator run a central AC or well pump?
Yes, but you must select a system capable of outputting **240V power** with a high continuous wattage. A standard 120V solar generator cannot run these devices. To power a well pump or central AC, you need an expandable system like the **EcoFlow Delta Pro** configured with a dual-voltage hub or wired directly into your home's breaker via a manual transfer switch or a Smart Home Panel.

### How long will a 3 kWh solar generator run my refrigerator?
A typical modern Energy Star refrigerator consumes roughly 100W to 150W of power while the compressor is actively running (averaging about 1 to 1.5 kWh per day). A high-performance **3.6 kWh EcoFlow Delta Pro** can easily run your refrigerator continuously for **2 to 3 days** on a single charge, even with zero solar input.
