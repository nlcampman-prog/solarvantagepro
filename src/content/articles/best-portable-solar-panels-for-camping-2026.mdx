## What to Look for in a Camping Solar Panel

1.  **Cell Type:** Monocrystalline is the standard for 2026. Avoid older polycrystalline cells, which are much less efficient.
2.  **IP Rating:** Look for at least **IP65** (water-resistant) or **IP68** (waterproof) if you plan on leaving your panels out overnight.
3.  **Connector Type:** Most panels use MC4 or DC7909 connectors. Ensure the panel you buy is compatible with your specific power station (adapters are available but can be a hassle).
4.  **Surface Material:** **ETFE** is superior to PET. It’s more durable, has better light transmission, and won't yellow or crack over time.
