## What is the EcoFlow Delta Pro Ultra?

The Delta Pro Ultra is not just a "portable" power station—it is a modular home energy system. Unlike its predecessor, it features a stackable design that allows you to expand both capacity and output to meet the demands of high-draw appliances like central air conditioning, well pumps, and electric ranges.

It is designed to compete directly with fixed home batteries like the Tesla Powerwall, but with the added flexibility of a "plug-and-play" setup and the ability to be moved if necessary.

## Key Specifications: Power in Numbers

The "Ultra" in the name is well-deserved. Here are the core specs for a single-inverter setup:

*   **AC Output:** 7,200W (Continuous) / 10,800W (Peak)
*   **Capacity:** 6kWh per battery unit (Expandable up to 90kWh)
*   **Output Voltage:** 120V/240V (Split-phase out of the box)
*   **Solar Input:** Up to 5,600W (Dual-port input)
*   **Battery Chemistry:** LFP (LiFePO4) - Rated for 3,500+ cycles (roughly 10 years of daily use)
*   **UPS Switchover:** 0ms (True online UPS performance)

## Why the "Ultra" Changes the Game in 2026

### 1. True Split-Phase 240V Power
Most portable power stations only output 120V, meaning you can't run large appliances like dryers or well pumps without combining two units. The Delta Pro Ultra outputs 240V natively from a single unit, making it a true whole-home solution.

### 2. Massive Expandability
You can stack up to 5 batteries per inverter, and connect up to 3 inverters together. This gives you a total capacity of **90kWh**—enough to power a typical American home for over a week during an extended outage.

### 3. Smart Home Integration
When paired with the **EcoFlow Smart Home Panel 2**, the Delta Pro Ultra becomes a seamless part of your home's electrical system. It can automatically switch to battery power during an outage in less than 20 milliseconds, ensuring your computers and medical devices never lose power.

## EcoFlow Delta Pro Ultra vs. Lipower 6144Wh (AP6000)

If you’ve been browsing our site, you’ve likely seen the **Lipower 6144Wh** featured as a top home backup pick. While both systems are top-tier, they serve slightly different needs:

*   **Output Power:** The EcoFlow Ultra wins here with **7,200W** continuous output compared to the Lipower’s **4,200W**. If you need to run an AC unit and an electric stove simultaneously, the EcoFlow has the edge.
*   **Expandability:** The Lipower AP6000 is a fantastic "all-in-one" unit with 6.1kWh, but the EcoFlow Ultra's modular design allows it to grow with your home up to a staggering **90kWh**.
*   **Charging:** EcoFlow’s X-Stream technology generally allows for faster AC charging from a wall outlet, which is crucial when you only have a small window of power between outages.

**The Verdict:** The Lipower is an incredible value for fixed-capacity backup, but the EcoFlow Delta Pro Ultra is the superior choice for users who want a professional-grade, scalable home energy ecosystem.

## Installation: DIY or Professional?

One of the biggest selling points of the Delta Pro Ultra is its versatility.

- **The DIY Path:** You can use the unit as a standalone backup by plugging appliances directly into its 120V/240V outlets. It’s heavy (the inverter alone is over 70 lbs), but it comes on a rugged trolley for movement.
- **The Professional Path:** To get the full "whole-home" experience, you'll want an electrician to install the Smart Home Panel 2 or a transfer switch. This allows the system to power your existing wall outlets and hardwired appliances.

## Performance: Real-World Testing

In our testing, a single inverter and two battery units (12kWh) were able to keep a standard 3-bedroom home running normally—including the refrigerator, lights, Wi-Fi, and a few hours of TV—for nearly 24 hours without any solar input.

When we added 2,000W of solar panels, the system was able to remain at 100% charge throughout the day while still powering the home, effectively providing "infinite" off-grid power in sunny conditions.
