## Brand Philosophy: Industrial vs. Consumer

### Goal Zero: The Rugged Pro
Goal Zero has always leaned toward the "professional" and "rugged" end of the spectrum. Their Yeti line is built with a heavy-duty aesthetic, often featuring metal casing and modular designs. They are the preferred choice for those who need a power station that can survive a job site or a multi-week off-grid expedition.

### Jackery: The User-Friendly King
Jackery, on the other hand, is the king of consumer-friendly design. Their Explorer series is iconic for its orange-and-black aesthetic, integrated handles, and "plug-and-play" simplicity. Jackery focuses on being lightweight and easy to use for the average camper or tailgater.

## Portability and Design

- **Jackery wins on weight.** They consistently produce power stations that are lighter than their Goal Zero equivalents, and their built-in wheels/telescoping handles on larger units are superior.
- **Goal Zero wins on durability.** The build quality of the Yeti line feels more "bomb-proof," and their modular ecosystem (Link and Tank expansions) allows you to build a true whole-home backup system.
